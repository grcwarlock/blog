title: On Writing Clearly
tag: Writing
lede: Most bad writing is not a vocabulary problem. It is a thinking problem.
---

Clear writing starts with clear thinking. If you cannot explain what you mean in a plain sentence, adding more words will not help. It will make things worse.

## Cut the filler

Every sentence should carry weight. If a sentence exists only to transition between two other sentences, delete it and let the gap do the work. Readers are smarter than most writing gives them credit for.

## Say the thing

The most common failure mode in writing is circling the point without landing on it. State the claim. Support it. Move on.

This applies whether you are writing a memo, a blog post, or an email. The reader's time is not yours to waste.

## Read it out loud

If a sentence sounds wrong when spoken, it reads wrong too. The ear catches what the eye skips — awkward rhythm, unnecessary repetition, clauses that go nowhere.

---

None of this is new advice. The hard part is doing it consistently, especially when the subject is complex and the temptation to hedge is strong.
